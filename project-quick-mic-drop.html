Overview

I identified a major disconnect in feedback systems: users had plenty of opinions, but feedback forms were so clunky and slow that almost no one bothered. Meanwhile, managers received responses with a 44 minute delay and had to manually sort and react by the time issues surfaced, they were old news.

I saw an opportunity: What if sharing feedback felt less like filling out paperwork and more like dropping a quick thought into a conversation?

That insight became Quick Mic Drop a mobile-first feedback flow with instant, humanized AI responses, sentiment-coded insights in Google Sheets, and automations for health and safety flags.

The goal was simple: Make users feel heard immediately while giving managers decision-ready signals they could act on in real time.

The Challenge

44-Min Response Delay

Users felt their input wasn't valued, causing them to disengage.

Manual Manager Burnout

Managers spent valuable time manually reading, categorizing, and responding to every submission, leading to burnout and delays.

No Feedback Loop

Users had no idea if their feedback was received or acted upon, which discouraged future submissions.

Critical Safety Risks

Delays in processing meant that food safety concerns, allergen issues, and operational problems were not escalated with urgency.

The Solution

My solution was to build a complete, end-to-end system designed to make users feel heard immediately, automate manual tasks for managers, and provide structured data for deeper analysis and actionable insights.

Conversational User UI & AI-Powered Response

The front-end delivers a lightweight, conversational, step-by-step flow that can be completed in about a minute.

  • Built a mobile-first, step-by-step UI with pill selectors and emoji sliders ‚Äî fast, intuitive, and Gen Z-friendly.
  • Added an optional "deep dive" so quick feedback didn't overwhelm casual users but power users could share more.
  • Used clear progress indicators to keep users engaged from start to finish.

An Automated Insight Engine

Behind the scenes, each submission automatically logs into a Google Sheet. An integrated OpenAI API, connected via Google Apps Script, instantly analyzes the sentiment (Positive, Neutral, Negative) and color-codes it in the sheet.

Real-Time Alert System

As new data is logged, an n8n automation triggers, scanning for high-risk keywords like "allergy," "dirty," or "food poisoning" within the feedback. Critical issues immediately trigger email alerts to the relevant teams.

The entire system was deliberately built using a simple, low-code stack (HTML/CSS/JS, Google Apps Script, Google Sheets, OpenAI, and n8n). This ensures our operations teams can easily maintain and adapt the system without requiring dedicated engineering resources.
